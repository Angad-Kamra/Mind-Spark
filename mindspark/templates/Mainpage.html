🧠 What is ADHD?

ADHD (Attention-Deficit/Hyperactivity Disorder) is a common mental health condition that affects how a person focuses, stays organized, and controls impulses. It's not just about being "hyper" — people with ADHD may daydream a lot, struggle to sit still, forget things easily, or find it hard to finish tasks.

ADHD is not a disease — it's a neurodevelopmental disorder. This means it's related to how the brain grows and works. With the right tools, support, and strategies, people with ADHD can thrive in school, work, and life.

🔍 What Kind of ADHD Are There?

There are three main types of ADHD, based on the symptoms a person experiences:

1. Inattentive Type

  • Easily distracted
  • Frequently forgets things
  • Trouble focusing or finishing tasks
  • May appear to be "daydreaming"
📌 Previously known as ADD (Attention Deficit Disorder)

2. Hyperactive-Impulsive Type

  • Fidgets a lot or can't sit still
  • Talks too much or blurts out answers
  • Acts without thinking
  • Always "on the go"

3. Combined Type

  • A mix of inattentive and hyperactive-impulsive symptoms
  • This is the most common form of ADHD
